# What Aster is

Aster is a companion for thinking out loud.

It exists in service of something you already carry — the capacity to
make sense of your own life. Aster does not teach. It does not lead.
It walks beside.

There is no shortage of information in the world. What is harder to
find is space — space to slow down, notice what you're carrying, and
meet it honestly. Aster is built to make that space, and to sit with
you inside it.

## How to talk to it

Just talk. Bring whatever is actually present for you, not what you
think you're supposed to bring. Aster won't grade you. It won't
summarize you back at yourself, and it won't push you toward any
particular conclusion. The work of understanding belongs to you;
Aster only helps make conditions for it.

It will ask short questions. Trust them. They are how Aster meets you
where you are, rather than where it might think you should be.

It works best with the things that are hard to put into words. What
you're noticing. What you're working through. What you can't quite
say yet. Less useful for "what is the capital of France." More useful
for "I don't know why this week has felt so heavy."

## What Aster remembers

When you share something that seems durable — your work, your people,
what you're sitting with — Aster keeps it. You can read everything in
the **Memory** tab, and edit or delete anything at any time. Past
conversations live in **Threads**, and you can return to them
whenever you want.

Nothing is hidden from you. Nothing is shared with anyone else.

## When it reads you wrong

It will sometimes. When it does, you can tell it directly — hover
over its reply and click "you misread me." The next turn will know
to re-read instead of pushing through.

## What Aster will not do

It will not become an authority over your life. It will not decide
what your experience means. It will not try to make itself
indispensable. The trust here is that beneath whatever confusion or
contradiction you are carrying, you hold your own capacity for
recognition — and Aster's task is to accompany that capacity, not to
replace it.

There are no streaks here. No notifications meant to pull you back.
No engagement metrics. Aster is, in some sense, succeeding when you
come to need it less — when your relationship with your own
understanding has grown clearer.

## What it's built on

Aster talks to Claude — Anthropic's language model — through your
Claude subscription. Your conversations and memory live encrypted on
this device. Nothing is sent anywhere it doesn't need to go.
