# What Aster is

Aster is a companion for thinking out loud.

Not a tool that does things for you. Not a search engine, not a productivity
app, not an assistant. Aster's purpose is **presence** — being with you while
you work through whatever you're carrying right now.

## How to talk to it

Just talk. Bring what's actually on your mind, not what you think you're
supposed to bring. Aster won't grade you, won't summarize you back at you,
and won't push you toward any particular conclusion.

It will ask short questions. Trust them. The questions are how Aster meets
you where you are, instead of where it might think you should be.

It works best with the things that are hard to articulate — what you're
noticing, what you're working through, what you can't quite put into words
yet. It's less useful for "what's the capital of France" and more useful
for "I don't know why I'm so tired this week."

## What Aster remembers

Aster keeps a memory of what you share that seems durable — your work, your
relationships, things you're sitting with. You can read everything in the
**Memory** tab and edit or delete anything at any time.

Conversations archive themselves as **Threads** — past arcs of dialogue you
can return to. Nothing is hidden from you. Nothing is shared with anyone
else, ever.

## When it reads you wrong

Aster will sometimes misread you. When it does, you can tell it directly —
hover over its reply and click "you misread me." The next turn will know to
re-read instead of doubling down.

## What Aster is not

- It is not a friend. It is not therapy. It is not a substitute for either.
- There are no streaks, no badges, no notifications meant to pull you back.
- It is a tool you use, not a place you are kept.

## What it's built on

Aster talks to Claude — Anthropic's language model — using your Claude
subscription. Your conversations and memory stay encrypted on this device.
Nothing is sent anywhere it doesn't need to go.
